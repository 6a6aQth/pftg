🌍 Dimba – Agricultural Precision and Digital Twin Platform
Concept Document
________________________________________
1. 📌 Overview
Dimba is an AI-powered precision agriculture and digital twin platform designed to transform farming in Malawi and across Africa.
The platform creates a living digital replica (digital twin) of each farm, integrating:
•	Geospatial mapping
•	Environmental data
•	Satellite intelligence
•	Agronomic models
to provide farmers with real-time insights, predictive analytics, and decision support.
________________________________________
2. 🎯 Vision
To build:
👉 Africa’s Agricultural Intelligence Layer
A platform where every farm has a digital twin that:
•	Understands its environment
•	Simulates outcomes
•	Guides decisions in real time
________________________________________
3. 🚨 Problem Statement
Farmers in Malawi face:
•	Unpredictable weather patterns
•	Inefficient use of inputs (fertilizer, water)
•	Limited access to localized agricultural data
•	Reliance on guesswork for critical decisions
This leads to:
•	Reduced yields
•	Increased costs
•	Food insecurity
________________________________________
4. 💡 Solution
Dimba provides:
👉 A Precision Agriculture Platform evolving into a Digital Twin System
that enables:
•	Real-time farm monitoring
•	Data-driven decision-making
•	Predictive and simulation-based insights
________________________________________
5. 🗺️ Farm Mapping & Spatial Intelligence
Users can:
•	Map farm boundaries using GPS
•	Divide farms into multiple plots
•	Assign crops to different zones
•	Manage multiple farms
Technologies such as Google Maps API and Mapbox enable:
•	Satellite visualization
•	Precise coordinate tracking
•	Interactive mapping
________________________________________
6. 🌦️ Environmental Intelligence Layer
Dimba integrates weather data from:
•	OpenWeather
•	NASA
Data captured:
•	Rainfall (amount + timing)
•	Temperature
•	Humidity
•	Solar radiation
•	Wind conditions
All data is tied to specific farm coordinates.
________________________________________
7. 🧪 Soil Intelligence System
7.1 Hybrid Soil Analysis
Dimba uses a combination of:
1. User Input
•	Soil type selection
2. AI Computer Vision (Soil Scan)
•	Soil image analysis via mobile camera
•	Detection of:
o	Soil color
o	Texture
o	Surface moisture
3. Future Integration
•	Soil testing services to enhance accuracy
________________________________________
7.2 Limitations
•	Nutrient composition (NPK, pH) cannot be fully derived from images
•	System provides probabilistic estimates, not lab-grade precision
________________________________________
